# Disc Brake Analysis and Topology Optimization

Academic Project | Rajiv Gandhi University of Knowledge Technologies  
Solo Project | Tools: CATIA V5, ANSYS Workbench

---

## 🎯 Objective

Conduct thermal and structural finite element analysis (FEA) of a disc brake assembly, 
then apply topology optimization to reduce component weight while maintaining structural 
integrity and improving heat dissipation performance.

---

## ⚙️ Methodology

### 1. CAD Modelling — CATIA V5
- Designed full disc brake geometry in CATIA V5 Part Design
- Modelled brake disc with ventilation holes and structural features

### 2. Structural Analysis — ANSYS Workbench
- Applied braking load conditions and constraints
- Evaluated von Mises stress distribution across disc surface
- Identified high-stress concentration zones

### 3. Thermal Analysis — ANSYS Workbench
- Simulated heat generation during braking event
- Analysed heat dissipation patterns across disc geometry
- Evaluated temperature distribution under peak braking load

### 4. Topology Optimization — ANSYS Workbench
- Set target: 60% material retention (40% reduction target)
- Removed non-load-bearing material in low-stress regions
- Validated optimized geometry against original stress and thermal results

---

## 📊 Results

| Metric | Original Design | Optimised Design |
|---|---|---|
| Weight reduction | — | ~10% |
| Structural integrity | Baseline | Maintained within safe limits |
| Heat dissipation | Baseline | Improved (increased surface area) |

Topology optimization successfully identified removable material in low-stress zones, 
achieving weight reduction while maintaining brake performance under simulated load.
